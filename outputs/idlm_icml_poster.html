Takeaway: transfer distillation from images to discrete text

Image diffusion already reduces long teacher chains using distillation. IDLM keeps that motivation, but moves it into the discrete language setting where token correlations cannot be ignored.

Image domain distill many teacher steps into a few student steps

Train the student with one clean signal: teacher loss minus fake loss.

3Teacher vs fake

Frozen teacher f⋆f^\star and trainable fake model f^\hat f explain the same student samples.

teacher f⋆f^\star

4Update student

The loss gap moves GθG_\theta toward samples that keep the teacher optimal.

LIDLM(θ) = L(f⋆,pθ) - minf^ L(f^,pθ) \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IDLM}}(\theta)=\mathcal{L}(f^\star,p_\theta)-\min_{\hat f}\mathcal{L}(\hat f,p_\theta)
Teacher beats fake model on student samples, so the student moves toward the teacher's data source.

Why this target is principled

LIDLM(θ) ≥ KL(pθ∥p⋆) ≥0 \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IDLM}}(\theta)\ge \mathrm{KL}(p_\theta\|p^\star)\ge 0
LIDLM(θ) =0 ⇔ pθ = p⋆ \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IDLM}}(\theta)=0 \Longleftrightarrow p_\theta=p^\star

For SEDD, MDLM, and Duo in the low-temperature limit, the population optimum is the data distribution.

Training recipe

A. SampleGenerate relaxed tokens with GθG_\theta.
B. CompareScore with f⋆f^\star and f^\hat f.
C. UpdateBackpropagate the loss gap.
