3. IDLM method

Theory: the inverse target is unique

For a broad class of discrete diffusion objectives, the IDLM loss
LIDLM (θ):= L(f*,pθ) − minf L(f,pθ) \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IDLM}}(\theta):=L(f^\star,p_\theta)-\min_f L(f,p_\theta)
satisfies:
LIDLM (θ)≥ DKL (pθ‖p*) ≥0 \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IDLM}}(\theta)\ge D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_\theta\|p^\star)\ge0
It achieves its minimum (zero) if and only if the model distribution matches the target distribution:
LIDLM (θ)=0 ⇔ pθ=p* \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{IDLM}}(\theta)=0\Longleftrightarrow p_\theta=p^\star

Discrete non-smoothness: train in the simplex

Sequence capacity: latent draws become sentence atoms

pθ (x01:L) = 𝔼ε [ ∏i=1L Cat(x0(i); Gθ(i)(ε)) ] p_\theta(x_0^{1:L})=\mathbb{E}_\epsilon\left[\prod_{i=1}^L\mathrm{Cat}(x_0^{(i)};G_\theta^{(i)}(\epsilon))\right]
